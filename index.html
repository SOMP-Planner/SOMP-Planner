SOMP: Stiffness Optimized Motion Planner

David Seyser  •  Micheal Schebek  •  Lucas Pammer
Paper Code

Abstract

Industrial robots offer greater flexibility and lower cost than CNC machines for milling applications, but suffer from low rigidity and vibration that compromise precision and surface quality. Existing motion planners typically rely on sampling-based methods that struggle with external forces and robot dynamics, while compliance is often handled as post-processing compensation rather than integrated into planning.

We propose SOMP (Stiffness Optimized Motion Planner), a novel framework that maintains maximum structural stiffness throughout robotic milling operations. Our approach samples inverse kinematics solutions at each CAM-generated waypoint and selects optimal configurations through multi-objective optimization considering manipulability, joint limits, and stiffness.

The stiffness objective is quantified using the Cartesian stiffness matrix \(\mathbf{K}_c = (\mathbf{J}\mathbf{K}_j^{-1}\mathbf{J}^T)^{-1}\), where \(\mathbf{J}\) is the geometric Jacobian and \(\mathbf{K}_j\) is the joint stiffness matrix. We evaluate SOMP against industry-standard motion planners in 2D milling operations (pocket milling and hole drilling) on aluminum, measuring shape tolerance, surface quality, vibration via accelerometers, and acoustic emissions via microphone. We hypothesize that stiffness optimization reduces vibrations and noise while improving surface finish and extending tool life, thereby enhancing the precision and feasibility of robotic machining for high-quality manufacturing.
